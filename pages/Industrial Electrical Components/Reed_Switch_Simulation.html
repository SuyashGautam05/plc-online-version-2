What is a Reed Switch?

A Reed Switch is a magnetically-operated electrical switch sealed inside a hermetic glass capsule. It contains two thin ferromagnetic metal reeds (contacts) that are normally separated (open). When a magnet comes close enough, the magnetic field magnetizes the reeds, causing them to attract and close the circuit.

Working Principle

The two reed contacts are made of a ferromagnetic alloy (usually iron-nickel). In the absence of a magnet, elastic spring force keeps the contacts apart — the switch is OPEN. When a permanent magnet or electromagnet is brought within the activation distance, the induced magnetism causes the reed tips to attract each other and snap CLOSED, completing the circuit. Removing the magnet causes the reeds to spring back open.

Magnetic Field — Key Insight

Only the area near the center of the glass capsule is sensitive to the magnetic field. The magnet must be aligned axially (poles along the tube axis, like N–S inline) for maximum activation force. Perpendicular orientation reduces the activation distance significantly.

PLC Ladder Logic (Left Side)

X0 is the Normally Open (NO) contact mapped to the Reed Switch input. When the magnet activates the reed switch, X0 closes, completing the rung and energizing the output coil Y0, which drives the indicator lamp output.

Wiring Circuit (Right Side)

The 24V DC supply is connected through the indicator lamp (Ind.) and power supply blocks (PS). The Reed Switch contact completes the circuit back to GND when a magnet brings the contacts together. No external power is needed by the reed switch itself — it simply acts as a mechanical contact.

Applications in PLC Systems

Reed switches are widely used in cylinder position sensing (pneumatic/hydraulic actuators), door/window position detection, liquid level float switches, conveyor home-position sensing, and any application where a simple non-contact magnetic trigger is required. They are especially popular in harsh environments since the contacts are sealed inside glass.

NO vs NC Reed Switch

A Normally Open (NO) reed switch (simulated here) has contacts that are open at rest and close when the magnet approaches. A Normally Closed (NC) reed switch has contacts that are permanently closed and open when the magnet approaches, used for failsafe detection circuits.
