What is a Pressure Sensor?

A pressure sensor (or pressure transmitter) is an instrument that measures the force exerted by a fluid (liquid or gas) on a surface per unit area. In industrial automation, it converts mechanical pressure into an electrical signal.

The standard output signal in PLC/SCADA systems is 4-20 mA, which represents a linear relationship between the measured pressure and the electrical output.

  • 4 mA → 0 psi (minimum pressure / live zero)
  • 20 mA → 150 psi (maximum pressure / full scale)
  • Live Zero (4 mA) helps detect broken wire vs zero pressure

How PLC Uses the Signal

The PLC (Programmable Logic Controller) reads the 4-20 mA current signal via an analog input module. It then converts this current value into engineering units (psi) using scaling functions.

  • Analog Input Module — Receives 4-20 mA signal
  • A/D Converter — Converts current to digital counts (e.g., 0-4095)
  • Scaling Block — Maps digital counts to psi
  • Setpoint Comparison — Triggers alarms or control actions
  • HMI/SCADA — Displays real-time value to operators

Types of Pressure Measurement

  • Gauge Pressure (PSIG) — Measured relative to atmospheric pressure (most common in industrial)
  • Absolute Pressure (PSIA) — Measured relative to perfect vacuum
  • Differential Pressure (PSID) — Difference between two pressure points (e.g., flow measurement)
  • Sealed Gauge — Referenced to atmospheric pressure at sea level

Working Principle — Bourdon Tube

The Bourdon tube is a C-shaped or coiled metal tube that straightens when internal pressure increases. This mechanical movement is converted to an electrical signal via:

  • Strain Gauge — Resistance changes with deformation
  • Piezoelectric — Generates voltage from pressure
  • Capacitive — Membrane deflection changes capacitance

The transmitter circuit amplifies this tiny signal and converts it to the standardized 4-20 mA loop current.

The 4-20 mA Formula — Complete Explanation

General Linear Formula
I = I_min + [(P − P_min) / (P_max − P_min)] × (I_max − I_min)
Simplified (0–150 psi)
mA = 4 + (P / 150) × 16
Reverse (mA → psi)
P = (I − 4) × (150 / 16)
P = (I − 4) × 9.375
Example: 12 mA
P = (12 − 4) × 9.375
P = 8 × 9.375 = 75 psi
Result: 75 psi (50% of range)

PLC Alarm Logic

The PLC continuously monitors the sensor output and compares it against programmed setpoints:

  • Low-Low (LL) — Sensor failure / empty vessel
  • Low (L) — Below normal operating range
  • Normal — Within acceptable range
  • High (H) — Above normal, operator alert
  • High-High (HH) — Critical, safety interlock activated

4-Wire vs 2-Wire Transmitter

  • 2-Wire (Loop Powered) — Power and signal on same two wires. Most common. Current loop: 4–20 mA powers the transmitter itself.
  • 4-Wire — Separate power supply and signal wires. Used for transmitters requiring more power (displays, complex electronics).

The 4 mA live zero is critical — if a wire breaks, current drops to 0 mA (not 4), so PLC can distinguish "no pressure" from "broken wire".
