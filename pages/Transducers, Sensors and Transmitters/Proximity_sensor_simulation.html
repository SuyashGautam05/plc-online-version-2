What is a Capacitive Proximity Sensor?

A capacitive proximity sensor detects objects by measuring changes in electrical capacitance. Unlike inductive sensors that only detect metals, capacitive sensors detect both metallic and non-metallic materials including plastics, wood, liquids, and powders.

Working Principle

The sensor face acts as one plate of a capacitor. When an object enters the detection field, it changes the dielectric constant, altering the capacitance. This triggers the internal oscillator circuit and activates the output signal.

PLC Ladder Logic (Left Side)

X0 is the Normally Open contact mapped to the capacitive sensor. When the sensor detects an object, X0 closes, energizing the output coil Y0, which drives the indicator lamp output.

Wiring Circuit (Right Side)

The 24V GND supply feeds 24V through the indicator lamp (Ind.) and power supply blocks (PS). The capacitive sensor contact (Cap. Sensor) completes the circuit back to GND when an object is detected.

Applications

Capacitive sensors are widely used in liquid level detection, packaging machines, conveyor systems, and food processing where both metallic and non-metallic objects must be sensed reliably.
