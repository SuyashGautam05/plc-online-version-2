RTD Temperature Sensor
What is an RTD?

An RTD (Resistance Temperature Detector) is a temperature sensor that measures temperature by correlating the resistance of a metal with temperature. The basic principle is that the electrical resistance of metals increases with temperature.

📦 Materials & Types

Common materials used in RTD construction:

  • Platinum — most common, widest range
  • Nickel — lower cost, narrow range
  • Copper — used for motor winding sensing

Platinum RTD (Pt100, Pt1000) is widely used due to stability, accuracy, and repeatability.

⚡ Working Principle

The working principle is based on the positive temperature coefficient (PTC) of metals:

  • When temperature increases, the metal's resistance increases.
  • This change in resistance is measured using an electrical circuit and converted into temperature.
Basic RTD Formula
R(T) = R₀ × (1 + α × ΔT)
R(T) = Resistance at temperature T
R₀ = Resistance at 0°C (100Ω for Pt100)
α = Temperature coefficient (~0.00385/°C for Pt)
ΔT = Change in temperature from 0°C

Measurement circuit configurations:

  • 2-wire RTD: Simple, affected by lead wire resistance
  • 3-wire RTD: Compensates for lead resistance
  • 4-wire RTD: Most accurate, eliminates lead errors

🔌 Voltage Divider Concept

An RTD changes its resistance with temperature. To measure that change as a voltage (easier for electronics), we use a voltage divider circuit.

Voltage Divider Formula
V_out = V_s × R_RTD / (R₁ + R_RTD)
V_s = Supply voltage (5V)
R_RTD = RTD resistance at temperature T
R₁ = Fixed series resistor
🧮 Example Calculation

Given:

  • Supply Voltage: V_s = 5V
  • Series Resistor: R₁ = 1000 Ω
  • RTD Type: PT100 at T = 100°C

Step 1 — Calculate RTD Resistance:

R(T) = R₀ × (1 + α × ΔT)
R(100°C) = 100 × (1 + 0.00385 × 100)
R(100°C) = 100 × 1.385 = 138.5 Ω

Step 2 — Calculate Voltage Output (V_out):

V_out = V_s × R_RTD / (R₁ + R_RTD)
V_out = 5 × 138.5 / (1000 + 138.5)
V_out = 692.5 / 1138.5
V_out = 0.608 V

As temperature increases → R_RTD increases → V_out increases → we can back-calculate temperature from V_out.
