Level Transmitter

Back to Course Simulation

A capacitive level transmitter is a type of level sensor that measures the level of liquids, solids, or slurries in a tank or vessel using the principle of capacitance change.

  • It is non-contact or contact-based, depending on the design.
  • Works well for liquids, powders, granules, or sticky substances.
  • Offers continuous level measurement, giving an analog signal (like 4-20 mA) to control systems.

Principle

Capacitance depends on the dielectric constant of the material between two conductive plates. When the level of the material changes, the dielectric constant changes, which changes the capacitance. This change is converted into an electrical signal proportional to the level.

Components

A typical capacitive level transmitter consists of:

  1. Probe or Sensor:
    • Acts as one plate of a capacitor.
    • Usually made of stainless steel or conductive material.
    • Can be a rod, cable, or plate depending on application.
  2. Reference Electrode:
    • Forms the other plate of the capacitor.
    • Often the tank wall itself serves as the reference plate.
  3. Electronics/Transmitter:
    • Measures capacitance changes.
    • Converts it into a standard electrical output (4–20 mA, voltage, or digital signal).
  4. Housing:
    • Protects the electronics from environmental factors like dust, moisture, or corrosive liquids.

Working

The working of a capacitive level transmitter can be summarized in a few steps:

  1. Capacitor Formation:
    • The probe and the reference electrode form a capacitor.
    • The dielectric between them is the process material (liquid, solid, slurry).
  2. Level Change Detection:
    • As the level of material changes, the dielectric constant between the plates changes.
    • This changes the capacitance of the sensor.
  3. Signal Conversion:
    • The capacitance change is measured by the electronics.
    • It is then converted into a proportional electrical signal.
  4. Output:
    • The transmitter outputs a continuous signal to a PLC, DCS, or display unit for monitoring and control.

Types of Capacitive Level Transmitters

  1. By Contact Type
    • Probe-type (contact): Probe touches the material directly (liquids, powders).
    • Non-contact (RF Capacitance / dielectric type): Sensor does not touch the material, suitable for corrosive or sticky substances.
  2. By Mounting
    • Rod or Stick Type: For solids or slurries.
    • Cable Type: Flexible, for tall tanks or silos.
    • Planar/Plate Type: Flat plates for narrow tanks or special applications.
  3. By Application
    • Single-point level detection: Detects high or low limits only.
    • Continuous level measurement: Provides real-time level measurement over the full range.

Applications

  • Chemical Industry: Measuring corrosive liquids or acids.
  • Food & Beverage: Monitoring grains, powders, or liquid ingredients.
  • Water & Wastewater: Tank level monitoring, sludge measurement.
  • Pharmaceuticals: Precise level measurement of powders or liquids.
  • Oil & Gas / Petrochemical: Measurement of oils, fuels, and other liquids.
  • Cement / Bulk Solids Handling: Measuring cement, sand, or granules in silos.
