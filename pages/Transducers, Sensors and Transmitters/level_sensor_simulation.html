What is a Capacitive Level Sensor?

A capacitive level sensor measures the dielectric change between two conductive plates (electrodes) as liquid rises between them. Since water has a dielectric constant (ε ≈ 80) much higher than air (ε ≈ 1), the capacitance changes linearly with the liquid level.

The transmitter converts this capacitance change into a standardized 4–20 mA signal that the PLC can process and display.

  • 4 mA → 0 mm (empty vessel — air between plates)
  • 20 mA → 250 mm (full vessel — water between plates)
  • Live zero (4 mA) allows broken-wire detection

Capacitance Formula — C = ε·A / d

The fundamental relationship governing capacitive sensors:

  • C = Capacitance (Farads)
  • ε = Dielectric constant of medium (air ≈ 1, water ≈ 80)
  • A = Area of electrode overlap (m²)
  • d = Distance between electrodes (m)

As water rises between the probe and vessel wall, ε increases dramatically (from ~1 to ~80), causing capacitance to rise proportionally. The transmitter electronics convert this change to 4–20 mA.

Level Measurement Formulas

Forward (mA → mm):
L = (I − 4) × 15.625

Reverse (mm → mA):
I = 4 + (L / 250) × 16

Hydrostatic (Pa):
P = ρ × g × h
P = 1000 × 9.81 × h(m)

Types of Capacitive Sensors

  • Bare/Uncoated Probe — Direct contact with liquid. Best for clean water, chemicals. Cannot use in conductive liquids.
  • Coated Probe (PTFE/PFA) — Insulating sleeve over electrode. Works with conductive liquids (water, acids). Most common in industry.
  • Non-contact — Measures through non-conductive tank wall. Used with aggressive chemicals or hygienic applications.
  • Differential — Two probes measure differential capacitance, compensating for density changes.

Hydrostatic Pressure — P = ρgh

Every column of liquid exerts pressure at its base due to gravity:

  • ρ (rho) = liquid density = 1000 kg/m³ for water
  • g = gravitational acceleration = 9.81 m/s²
  • h = height of liquid column (meters)

Example — 250 mm of water:
h = 0.250 m
P = 1000 × 9.81 × 0.250 = 2452.5 Pa

PLC Alarm Logic for Level

  • Low-Low (LL) < 20 mm — Pump shutdown to prevent cavitation / dry running
  • Low (L) < 40 mm — Open inlet valve, alert operator
  • Normal 40–200 mm — System operating correctly
  • High (H) > 200 mm — Close inlet valve, alert operator
  • High-High (HH) > 235 mm — Emergency shutdown, overflow alarm
