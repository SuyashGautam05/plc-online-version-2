Profinet

Profinet (Process Field Network) is an industrial Ethernet communication protocol developed by Siemens as the successor to Profibus. It enables high-speed, real-time data exchange between automation devices such as PLCs, VFDs, sensors, actuators, and HMIs.

Unlike Profibus, which is fieldbus-based, Profinet uses standard Ethernet, combining the advantages of Ethernet networks with industrial deterministic performance.

Key Features of Profinet

  • Ethernet-based (100 Mbps or more)
  • Real-time and isochronous communication for automation and motion control
  • Deterministic data transfer for fast control loops
  • Supports large device networks
  • Seamless integration with IT networks and Industry 4.0 systems
  • Uses standard TCP/IP for non-time-critical data

Types of Profinet Communication

1. Profinet RT (Real-Time)

  • For standard industrial automation
  • Cyclic data transfer between controllers and devices
  • Fast response time (~1 ms cycle)

Applications:

  • VFD control
  • PLC–I/O communication
  • Sensors and actuators in production lines

2. Profinet IRT (Isochronous Real-Time)

  • For motion control and robotics
  • Extremely low jitter and precise timing
  • Cycle times can be less than 1 ms
  • Ensures synchronized motion across multiple axes

Applications:

  • CNC machines
  • Multi-axis robotics
  • Packaging and assembly machines

3. Profinet CBA (Component-Based Automation) – less common now

  • Modular system design for distributed automation components
  • Mainly replaced by RT and IRT in modern systems

Advantages of Profinet

  • High-speed communication (100 Mbps or more)
  • Deterministic and real-time control
  • Supports plug-and-play device replacement
  • Scalable for small to very large automation systems
  • Integrates easily with IT systems and Industry 4.0
  • Reduces wiring compared to traditional fieldbuses

Profinet vs Profibus (Quick Comparison)

Feature Profibus Profinet
Medium Serial fieldbus Ethernet
Speed Up to 12 Mbps 100 Mbps or more
Real-Time DP/PA deterministic RT/IRT deterministic
Integration Limited with IT Full TCP/IP & IT integration
Topology Line, tree, star Star, line, ring
Use Traditional factory & process automation Modern industrial automation, motion control, Industry 4.0

Typical Applications of Profinet

  • PLC → VFD communication in high-speed processes
  • Motion control and robotics
  • Factory automation: packaging, conveyor systems, CNC
  • Large-scale distributed automation networks

Working Principle – Example: PLC to VFD via Profinet

  1. PLC acts as Profinet controller (master).
  2. VFD acts as Profinet device (slave).
  3. PLC sends speed and torque setpoints to VFD over Profinet RT.
  4. VFD returns feedback signals (motor current, speed, status) in real-time.
  5. IRT can be used if multiple axes require synchronized motion.
